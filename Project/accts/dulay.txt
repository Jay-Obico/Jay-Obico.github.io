DULAY, ANN E.
Associate Professor
Electronics And Communications Engineering
Dr. Ann E. Dulay is an Associate Professor at De La Salle University. She finished her BSECE degree from the University of the East in 1989. She pursued and completed her MSECE degree at De La Salle University in 2000 where she also finished her PhD in 2017. She started teaching in 1991 and taught various courses to include analog and digital electronics, IC design, computer aided design, methods of research, safety engineering, ECE laws and ethics. Her research interests include power line communications, FPGA and microcontroller-based systems, microelectronics, smart cities, internet-of-things, and disaster resiliency. Dr. Dulay is a Professional Electronics Engineer and an active member and former officer of the Institute of Electronics Engineers of the Philippines (IECEP). She is also a member of IEEE and has presented/published research outputs in various conferences and journals.
